Cocaine

The immediate effects of Cocaine:

Taking Cocaine has a few immediate effects, these include:

  • A short-lived, intense high (followed by intense depression, edginess and craving for more of the drug)
  • Greatly increased heart rate, muscle spasms and convulsions
  • Paranoia, anger, hostility and anxiety (even when not high)
  • Contracted blood vessels
  • Increased rate of breathing
  • Disturbed eating and sleeping patterns
  • Nausea
  • Hallucinations
  • Panic and psychosis

The long term effects of Cocaine:

Taking Cocaine has a few long term effects, these include:

  • A necessity to take greater and greater quantities for the same 'high'
  • Sleep deprivation, loss of appetite
  • Hallucinations, disorientation
  • Necessity to take more and more of the drug to feel 'normal'
  • Addiction so strong as to a loss of interest in other aspects of life
  • Sever depression (leading to doing anything to get more of the drug)
  • Permanent damage to blood vessels of heart and brain
  • High blood pressure, heart attacks, strokes, death.
  • Malnutrition, weight loss, severe tooth decay
  • Sexual problems, reproductive damage, infertility

Agencies for help:

Australia has several agencies that are available for people wanting help. Some of these are:

Family Drug Support 1300 368 186 24 hours/day, 7 days/week Support for families faced with problematic drug use.

DirectLine 1800 888 236 24 hours/day, 7 days/week Confidential alcohol and drug counselling and referral line.

Laws and penalties regarding Cocaine

Cocaine is illegal in Australia. Our laws distinguish between those who use drugs and those who supply or traffic drugs.

  • In Victoria, you could receive a $2000 fine and/or one year of imprisonment for the cultivation of Cocaine
  • In Victoria, you could receive a $3000 fine and/or one year of imprisonment for possession/use to $250,000 and/or 25 years of imprisonment for commercial trafficking.

Marijuana

The immediate effects of Marijuana:

Taking Marijuana has a few immediate effects, these include:

  • Sensory distortion
  • Panic
  • Anxiety
  • Poor coordination of movement
  • A lowered reaction time
  • An increased heartbeat (with a risk of a heart attack)

The long term effects of Marijuana:

Taking Marijuana has a few long term effects, these include:

  • A reduced resistance to common illnesses (e.g. colds)
  • A suppression of the immune system
  • Growth disorders
  • Reduction of male sex hormones
  • Rapid destruction of lung fibers
  • Apathy, drowsiness, lack of motivation, etc.
  • Personality and mood changes
  • Inability to understand things clearly
